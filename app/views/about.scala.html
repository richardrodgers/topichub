About TopicHub

TopicHub is a web-based service to facilitate distribution of packaged content, using a publish/subscribe model. Packages may contain any number of files, but the service typically would be used to distribute a primary artifact (e.g. a scholarly article), together with metadata or other supplementary files. Publishers and subscribers are expected to be primarily institutional or commercial entities, but the service could also be used by individuals on either end.

The service embraces open content interoperability standards wherever possible, specifically:

  • Content transfers from publishers to the hub and from the hub to subscribers utilize the SWORD (v1.3) protocol.
  • Metadata for content residing on the hub is exposed on an Open Archives Initiative (version 2.0) OAI-PMH data provider endpoint here
  • Content residing on the hub may be harvested using the NISO/OAI ResourceSync (draft) protocols.
  • In application web pages, metadata is expressed using the Schema.org vocabulary.
  • Topics identified by the hub are syndicated as Atom feeds containing items.

TopicHub is built upon an open-source, Apache 2 licensed platform available on GitHub.
